About Abdul Kalam

Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, was an esteemed Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002-2007). Known as the "Missile Man of India," he significantly advanced India's missile and nuclear technology, including the Pokhran-II tests. He worked at DRDO and ISRO, notably leading the SLV-III project. Honored with the Bharat Ratna in 1997, Kalam was celebrated for his humility and commitment to education. Post-presidency, he inspired millions through his lectures and writings. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving a lasting legacy.
